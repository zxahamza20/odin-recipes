Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant, spiced rice dish that originated in South Asia and is widely enjoyed across India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and beyond. It is traditionally made by layering long-grain basmati rice with marinated meat such as chicken, lamb, or beef, or sometimes vegetables, along with yogurt, onions, garlic, ginger, and a rich blend of spices like saffron, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. The dish is slow-cooked so the flavors meld together, giving biryani its signature aroma and depth.

What makes biryani special is the variety of regional styles, such as Hyderabadi, Lucknowi, and Sindhi biryani, each with its own balance of spices and cooking techniques. Often served with raita or salad, biryani is both a celebratory and comfort food, commonly prepared for festivals, family gatherings, and special occasions.

Ingredients

Main Components

  • Basmati rice
  • Meat (chicken, lamb/mutton, beef) or vegetables (potato, carrots, peas, cauliflower, paneer)
  • Water or stock

Marinade

  • Yoghurt
  • Ginger PAste
  • Garlic Paste
  • Lemon Juice
  • Salt

Whole spices

  • Bay Leaves
  • Green Cardamom
  • Black Cardamom
  • Cloves
  • Cinnamon Sticks
  • Star Anise
  • Cumin Seeds
  • Black peppercorns

Ground spices

  • Red Chilli Powder
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Coriander Powder
  • Garam Masala
  • Kashmiri Chilli Powder (for color)

Aromatics & Herbs

  • Onions (thinly sliced, often fried)
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro)
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Green chilies

Fats & Flavor Enhancers

  • Ghee
  • Cooking Oil
  • Saffron (warm milk soaked)
  • Rose Water or Kewra Water

Addons

  • Potatoes
  • Boiled Eggs
  • Fresh Cashews or Raisins

Steps

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice, then parboil it with whole spices and drain.
  2. Marinate meat or vegetables with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, spices, salt, and lemon juice.
  3. Fry sliced onions in oil or ghee until golden brown and set aside.
  4. Cook the marinated meat or vegetables until partially done and fragrant.
  5. Layer the cooked meat/vegetables and rice in a pot, adding fried onions, mint, coriander, saffron milk, and ghee.
  6. Seal the pot and cook on low heat (dum) until the rice is fully cooked and flavors are absorbed.
  7. Gently fluff the biryani and serve hot with raita or salad.
