Pad Kee Mow

Description

Pad kee mow, often called “drunken noodles,” is a popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish known for its bold, savory flavors. It’s typically made with wide rice noodles, garlic, chili, and a mix of vegetables like Chinese broccoli, bell peppers, and baby corn, along with protein such as chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu. The sauce combines soy sauce, oyster sauce, and fish sauce, creating a rich, salty, and slightly sweet base.

What sets pad kee mow apart is its fragrant use of Thai basil and its spicy kick, which gives the dish a fresh, aromatic finish. Despite the nickname “drunken noodles,” there’s no alcohol in the recipe—the name is commonly said to come from the idea that the dish pairs well with drinks or cures a late-night craving. The result is a hearty, spicy, and satisfying meal that’s a staple in Thai cuisine.

Ingredients

Noodles & Protein

  • Wide Rice Noodles
  • Chicken, Beef, Shrimp, Tofu

vegetables & Aromatics

  • Garlic
  • Thai Chillies (or other hot Chillies)
  • Chinese Broccoli (gai lan)
  • Baby Corn
  • Bell Peppers
  • onion or Shallots

Herbs

  • Thai Basil

Sauces & Seasonings

  • Soy sauce (often dark soy sauce)
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Fish Sauce
  • Sugar (White or Palm)

Cooking Oil

  • Vegetable oil or another neutral oil

Extras in variations

  • Mushrroms
  • Carrots
  • Green Beans

Steps

  1. Prepare all ingredients by slicing the protein, chopping vegetables, mincing garlic, and cooking or loosening the wide rice noodles.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over high heat until hot.
  3. Add garlic and chilies and stir-fry briefly until fragrant.
  4. Add the protein and cook until just done.
  5. Toss in vegetables and stir-fry until slightly tender but still crisp.
  6. Add the rice noodles and stir to combine.
  7. Pour in soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar and toss until noodles are evenly coated.
  8. Add a splash of water if needed to loosen the noodles and prevent sticking.
  9. Turn off the heat and fold in Thai basil until just wilted.
  10. Taste, adjust seasoning if needed, and serve immediately.
